Maternal Health

🌸 Mom
Mnemonic: B-U-B-B-L-E-H-E — systematic head-to-toe postpartum assessment

B — Breasts

  • Softness, engorgement, symmetry
  • Nipple condition: cracks, flat, inverted
  • Colostrum/milk presence
  • Breast/latching education offered

U — Uterus

  • Fundal height (fingerbreadths below umbilicus)
  • Firmness: boggy → massage needed
  • Midline position
  • Day 1: at umbilicus; involutes ~1 cm/day
⚠ Alert: Boggy uterus → assess bladder, then fundal massage. Report excessive bleeding immediately.

B — Bowel

  • Bowel sounds in all 4 quadrants
  • Last BM documented
  • Stool softeners ordered/given?
  • Passing flatus; no distension

B — Bladder

  • Voiding adequately (≥150 mL per void)
  • Bladder not distended above symphysis
  • U/O ≥30 mL/hr (post-Foley)
  • Pain/burning with urination

L — Lochia

  • Color: Rubra (red, 1–3d) → Serosa (pink/brown, 4–10d) → Alba (cream, 10–14d)
  • Amount: scant / light / moderate / heavy / excessive
  • Odor: foul odor = infection concern
  • Clots: >golf ball size = report

E — Episiotomy / Perineum

  • REEDA: Redness, Edema, Ecchymosis, Discharge, Approximation
  • Ice pack first 24 hrs, sitz bath after
  • Intact sutures (if present)
  • Hemorrhoids noted

H — Homans / Lower Extremities

  • Calf pain on dorsiflexion (Homans sign — limited sensitivity)
  • Leg edema, warmth, erythema
  • DVT risk: early ambulation, SCDs

E — Emotional / Education

  • Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) if applicable
  • Baby blues vs. PPD distinction
  • Support system, bonding observed
  • Discharge education completed

Pain Management

  • Ibuprofen 600 mg PO q6h (preferred — anti-inflammatory)
  • Acetaminophen 650–1000 mg PO q6h PRN
  • Ice perineum first 24h; sitz bath after
  • Tucks pads / Dermoplast spray for perineal comfort
  • Afterpains: worse with multiparas + breastfeeding; reassure

Routine Medications

💊 Prenatal vitamin 🩸 Iron supplement 💉 Pitocin IV (if ordered) 🟡 Stool softener 🛡 Rhogam (Rh neg only) 💉 Rubella vaccine (if non-immune)

Hemorrhage Prevention

  • Fundal massage if boggy
  • Oxytocin (Pitocin) — standard uterotonic
  • Methergine (ergotamine) — NOT with HTN
  • Hemabate — NOT with asthma
  • Monitor CBC, report Hgb <8 g/dL

Early Discharge Teaching

  • Pelvic rest × 6 weeks
  • No heavy lifting (>10 lb) × 4–6 weeks
  • Signs of infection: fever >100.4°F (38°C), foul lochia, wound opening
  • Signs of PPD; 24/7 support line provided
  • OB follow-up: 3 weeks C/S or 6 weeks vaginal
  • Contraception counseling
Tip: Teach back the warning signs — lochia changes, fever >100.4°F (38°C), chest pain, SOB, and call-when-to-worry.

Key Teaching Points

  • Feed every 1.5–3 hours, 8–12×/day newborn
  • Alternate breasts; fully empty one side first
  • Signs of effective feeding: audible swallowing, satisfied infant, weight gain
  • Nipple pain is NOT normal; reassess latch
  • Engorgement: nurse frequently, warm compress before, cool after
  • Mastitis signs: flu-like symptoms + unilateral breast warmth/redness

Colostrum → Milk Transition

Day 1–3: Colostrum 🌕 Day 3–5: Transitional milk Day 7–10: Mature milk 🍼

Head-to-Toe Physical Exam

  • Head: fontanelles (anterior large, posterior small), molding, caput/cephalhematoma
  • Eyes: sclera clear, no discharge, red reflex present bilaterally
  • Ears: auricle formation, position (top at outer canthus of eye)
  • Mouth: intact palate, Epstein pearls (normal), sucking reflex
  • Clavicles: palpate for fracture (esp. after shoulder dystocia)
  • Chest: equal bilateral breath sounds, no retractions/grunting
  • Abdomen: cord: 2 arteries + 1 vein; soft, no masses
  • Genitalia: normal for gestational age, patent anus
  • Extremities: 10 fingers + toes, equal Moro, grasp reflexes
  • Hips: Ortolani / Barlow maneuver — no clunk/click
  • Skin: color, vernix, lanugo, Mongolian spots (document!)

Reflexes to Assess

Rooting Sucking Moro (startle) Palmar grasp Plantar grasp Babinski Stepping Tonic neck (fencing)

Newborn Screenings

  • Newborn metabolic screen (state PKU card) — at 24h or before discharge
  • Hearing screen (UNHS) — before discharge
  • Critical CHD screen: pulse ox pre/post ductal at 24h+
  • Bilirubin — TCB or serum per protocol
  • Blood glucose per protocol (risk infants)

Weight Changes

  • Expected weight loss: up to 7–10% birth weight in first 3–5 days
  • >10% loss = evaluate feeding, consider supplementation
  • Should regain birth weight by day 10–14
  • Gaining ~20–30 g/day after transitional loss

Safe Sleep (AAP Guidelines)

  • Back to sleep, EVERY time — supine position
  • Firm, flat sleep surface (approved crib/bassinet only)
  • Room-sharing (not bed-sharing) for ≥6 months
  • No soft objects, pillows, bumpers, or loose bedding
  • Keep room temp comfortable — avoid overheating
  • Pacifier at sleep time (after BF established)
  • No smoking, alcohol, or drug exposure environment
⚠ SIDS Risk Reduction: "Back to sleep, tummy to play." Document safe sleep teaching every shift.

Cord Care

  • Keep stump clean and dry
  • Fold diaper below cord stump to air-dry
  • Sponge baths only until cord falls off (1–3 weeks)
  • Normal: dries → shrinks → falls off; may have mild odor
  • Abnormal: redness extending onto abdomen, foul smell, bleeding, discharge → report

Circumcision Care (if applicable)

  • Yellow exudate on glans = normal healing (fibrin)
  • Apply petroleum jelly to gauze with each diaper change × 24h
  • Monitor for: bleeding >2 tsp, no urine × 12h, signs of infection
  • Plastibell: ring falls off in 5–8 days; do not pull

Jaundice / Hyperbilirubinemia

  • Physiologic jaundice: appears day 2–3, peaks day 3–5, resolves by day 14
  • Pathologic: appears <24h of life → investigate immediately
  • Assess cephalocaudal progression (face → chest → abdomen → legs)
  • Treatment: phototherapy (bili lights); hydration; rarely exchange transfusion
  • BF jaundice: increase feeds → 8–12×/day to increase stooling
Bilirubin threshold (term, healthy)
Use Bhutani nomogram — action based on age in hours + risk factors

Thermoregulation

  • Newborns cannot shiver — prone to heat loss
  • 4 mechanisms of heat loss: Evaporation, Radiation, Conduction, Convection
  • Warm, dry delivery room; dry infant immediately after birth
  • Skin-to-skin with mom = best thermoregulation
  • Hat on head — largest area of heat loss
  • Target axillary temp: 97.7–99.5°F (36.5–37.5°C)

Routine Medications at Birth

💉 Erythromycin eye ointment 💉 Vitamin K 1 mg IM 🛡 Hep B vaccine (if consent)
