Maternal Health

🌸 Mom
🩺Maternal Assessment (BUBBLE-HE)
Mnemonic: B-U-B-B-L-E-H-E — systematic postpartum assessment

B — Breasts

  • Softness, engorgement, symmetry
  • Nipple condition: cracks, flat, inverted
  • Colostrum/milk presence
  • Latching education offered

U — Uterus

  • Fundal height (fingerbreadths below umbilicus)
  • Firmness: boggy → massage needed
  • Midline position; involutes ~1 cm/day
⚠ Boggy uterus: assess bladder first, then fundal massage.

B — Bowel

  • Bowel sounds in all 4 quadrants
  • Last BM documented; stool softeners ordered?
  • Passing flatus; no distension

B — Bladder

  • Voiding adequately (≥150 mL per void)
  • Bladder not distended above symphysis
  • U/O ≥30 mL/hr post-Foley

L — Lochia

  • Rubra (red,1–3d) → Serosa (pink,4–10d) → Alba (cream,10–14d)
  • Foul odor = infection; clots >golf ball = report

E — Episiotomy / Perineum

  • REEDA: Redness, Edema, Ecchymosis, Discharge, Approximation
  • Ice pack first 24h; sitz bath after; Tucks/Dermoplast

H — Homans / Lower Extremities

  • Calf pain on dorsiflexion; leg edema, warmth, erythema
  • DVT risk: early ambulation, SCDs

E — Emotional / Education

  • Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS)
  • Baby blues vs. PPD; support system; DC education complete
💊Postpartum Care

Pain Management

  • Ibuprofen 600 mg PO q6h (preferred)
  • Acetaminophen 650–1000 mg q6h PRN
  • Ice perineum 24h; sitz bath after; Tucks/Dermoplast
  • Afterpains worse with multiparas + breastfeeding

Routine Medications

💊 Prenatal vitamin🩸 Iron supplement💉 Pitocin IV🟡 Stool softener🛡 Rhogam (Rh neg)💉 Rubella (non-immune)

Hemorrhage Prevention

  • Fundal massage if boggy; Oxytocin (Pitocin) standard
  • Methergine — NOT with HTN; Hemabate — NOT with asthma
  • Report Hgb <8 g/dL

Discharge Teaching

  • Pelvic rest × 6 weeks; no lifting >10 lb × 4–6 wks
  • Fever >100.4°F, foul lochia, wound opening → call OB
  • OB follow-up: 3 wks C/S, 6 wks vaginal

Staged Response

Stage 1EBL 500–999 mL
  1. 1Call for help — notify charge nurse & OB provider immediately
  2. 2Quantify blood loss (QBL) — weigh all pads and chux
  3. 3Fundal massage — firm, sustained; assess for atony
  4. 4Ensure IV access — 18g or larger; run fluids wide open
  5. 5Bladder: empty via straight cath if patient hasn't voided
  6. 6Vital signs q15 min; continuous pulse ox
  7. 7Draw STAT labs: CBC, BMP, coags (PT/PTT/INR/fibrinogen), T&S
First-line meds
Oxytocin 10–40 units IV drip Sustained fundal massage Warm blankets — keep patient warm
Stage 2EBL 1000–1499 mL
  1. 1Activate PPH protocol / massive transfusion protocol if available
  2. 2Second large-bore IV access — 16g or larger
  3. 3Oxygen 10 L/min via non-rebreather mask
  4. 4Insert Foley catheter — strict UO monitoring (goal ≥30 mL/hr)
  5. 5Bimanual uterine compression if atony persists
  6. 6Notify anesthesia; prepare for possible OR transfer
  7. 7Warm IV fluids: LR or NS 1–2 L bolus
  8. 8VS q5–10 min; document all blood products given
Second-line meds
Methergine 0.2 mg IM (not if HTN) Hemabate 250 mcg IM q15–90 min (not if asthma) Cytotec 800–1000 mcg PR or SL
⚠ Methergine contraindicated in hypertension. Hemabate contraindicated in asthma/COPD.
Stage 3EBL ≥1500 mL or unstable
  1. 1CALL CODE / Emergency response — all hands on deck now
  2. 2Initiate massive transfusion — pRBC : FFP : Plt ratio 1:1:1
  3. 3Tranexamic acid (TXA) 1 g IV over 10 min — must give within 3 hrs of birth
  4. 4Position supine, legs elevated; aggressive warming
  5. 5Arterial line — anesthesia places
  6. 6Surgical options: B-Lynch suture, uterine artery ligation, Bakri balloon
  7. 7Prepare OR for possible hysterectomy
  8. 8Continuous VS; document every intervention with exact time
Emergency meds & blood products
TXA 1 g IV over 10 min pRBC — O-neg if no type available FFP 1:1 with pRBC Cryoprecipitate if fibrinogen <200 Calcium gluconate 1 g IV per 4 units pRBC
TXA must be given within 3 hours of delivery — effectiveness drops significantly after. Can repeat at 30 min if bleeding continues.
Stage 4Cardiovascular collapse
  1. 1CPR if pulseless — call code blue immediately
  2. 2Emergency hysterectomy — definitive hemorrhage control
  3. 3Vasopressors per anesthesia orders
  4. 4ICU transfer after stabilization
Vasopressors per anesthesia Continue massive transfusion Correct coagulopathy aggressively

Document Every Step

  • Time bleeding noted + estimated volume at each assessment
  • Every intervention with exact time — massage, meds, IV, O₂
  • All blood products: type, unit #, time started/ended, RN initials
  • Vital signs with timestamps — q5 min during active hemorrhage
  • Provider notifications: who, what time, what was communicated
  • Patient response to each intervention
  • Running cumulative QBL total
SBAR handoff: Situation → Background → Assessment → Recommendation. State clearly: "I am concerned this patient is hemorrhaging."

Nursing Actions by Severity

Mild HTNBP ≥140/90
  1. 1Repeat BP in 15 min, same arm, patient at rest
  2. 2Notify provider — document exact time
  3. 3Assess: headache, visual changes, RUQ pain, edema
  4. 4Ensure IV access; review orders
  5. 5Labs if ordered: CBC, CMP, LFTs, uric acid, urine protein
  6. 6Increase BP monitoring frequency
Severe HTNBP ≥160/110
  1. 1Call provider IMMEDIATELY — time sensitive emergency
  2. 2Antihypertensive must be given within 30–60 min
  3. 3Large-bore IV × 2; O₂ if sat <95%
  4. 4Continuous BP q5–10 min until stable
  5. 5Foley catheter — strict I&Os; goal UO ≥30 mL/hr
  6. 6Prepare mag sulfate if not already running
  7. 7Seizure precautions: padded rails, suction ready, dim lights
  8. 8Keep room quiet — reduce stimulation
🚨 Call Provider / Rapid Response NOW if:
  • BP ≥160/110 on two readings 15 min apart
  • Sudden severe headache or visual changes (flashing lights, blurred vision)
  • Seizure activity — call code, give mag, protect airway
  • RUQ or epigastric pain with nausea — possible HELLP
  • Pulmonary edema: dyspnea, O₂ sat dropping, frothy sputum
  • Platelets dropping rapidly or <50,000
  • Altered mental status or sudden loss of consciousness
  • Mag toxicity: RR <12, absent DTRs, UO <25 mL/hr

Eclampsia — If Seizure Occurs

  1. 1Call for help — do NOT leave patient
  2. 2Position left lateral — protect airway, do NOT restrain
  3. 3O₂ 8–10 L/min via face mask
  4. 4Give mag sulfate 4–6 g IV loading if not already on it
  5. 5If already on mag: give additional 2 g IV slow push
  6. 6Document: time of onset, duration, activity, post-ictal state
  7. 7Prepare for emergency delivery if antepartum
Seizure precautions for ALL preeclampsia patients: padded rails up, suction at bedside, call light within reach, quiet environment.

Head-to-Toe

  • Head: fontanelles, molding, caput/cephalhematoma
  • Eyes: sclera clear, red reflex bilateral
  • Mouth: intact palate, Epstein pearls ok, sucking reflex
  • Chest: equal breath sounds, no retractions/grunting
  • Abdomen: cord 2 arteries + 1 vein; soft, no masses
  • Hips: Ortolani/Barlow — no clunk/click
  • Skin: color, vernix, Mongolian spots (document!)

  • PKU/metabolic screen at 24h or before discharge
  • Hearing screen (UNHS) before discharge
  • Critical CHD: pulse ox pre/post ductal at 24h+

🌙General Infant Care

Safe Sleep (AAP)

  • Back to sleep EVERY time — supine
  • Firm, flat surface (approved crib/bassinet only)
  • Room-sharing (not bed-sharing) ≥6 months
  • No soft objects, pillows, bumpers, loose bedding
⚠ SIDS: "Back to sleep, tummy to play." Document every shift.

Jaundice

  • Physiologic: day 2–3, peaks day 3–5, resolves day 14
  • Pathologic: appears <24h → investigate immediately
  • Cephalocaudal: face → chest → abdomen → legs
  • Treatment: phototherapy, hydration

Thermoregulation

  • Newborns cannot shiver — prone to heat loss
  • 4 mechanisms: Evaporation, Radiation, Conduction, Convection
  • Skin-to-skin = best thermoregulation

Routine Meds at Birth

💉 Erythromycin eye ointment 💉 Vitamin K 1 mg IM 🛡 Hep B vaccine (if consent)

TCB vs TSB — When to Escalate

📟 TCB (Transcutaneous)
  • Non-invasive screening tool
  • Use for routine monitoring
  • Reliable when <13 mg/dL
  • Less accurate after phototherapy
🩸 TSB (Serum)
  • Gold standard — always confirms
  • Use when TCB ≥13 or near threshold
  • Required before starting phototherapy
  • Use after phototherapy stopped

Risk Factors for Severe Hyperbilirubinemia

ABO incompatibility Rh incompatibility G6PD deficiency Cephalohematoma / bruising Significant weight loss >10% Poor feeding / low intake East Asian / Mediterranean descent Sibling with jaundice history Gestational age 35–37 wks

Phototherapy Care Steps

  1. 1Apply eye shields snugly — check q2h for proper fit and skin integrity
  2. 2Maximize skin exposure — diaper only; remove hat and clothing
  3. 3Bili light distance per device specs (conventional ~15–20 cm; LED closer)
  4. 4Reposition q2–4h — supine and prone alternating
  5. 5Feed q2–3h minimum — adequate intake drives bilirubin excretion
  6. 6Temp check q4h — bili lights can cause hypo or hyperthermia
  7. 7Repeat TSB per order (typically q4–6h during active phototherapy)
  8. 8Brief breaks for feeding/bonding are OK — document off-light time
  9. 9Rebound TSB 12–24h after stopping phototherapy before discharge
Bili blanket (fiberoptic): Can be used during feeding and kangaroo care — no eye shields needed. Good for outpatient or supplement to overhead light.
🚨 Exchange Transfusion — Call NICU NOW if:
  • TSB at or above exchange threshold for gestational age (see table above)
  • TSB rising >0.5 mg/dL/hr despite phototherapy
  • Signs of acute bilirubin encephalopathy: lethargy, hypotonia, high-pitched cry, arching (opisthotonus), fever
  • Positive DAT (Coombs) + rapidly rising TSB

Parent Teaching Points

  • Jaundice is common — appears in ~60% of healthy newborns; most resolves on its own
  • Feed frequently (8–12×/day) — feeds help baby poop out bilirubin
  • Bili lights are safe; brief breaks for feeding and skin-to-skin are encouraged
  • Watch for: baby too sleepy to wake for feeds, yellow past abdomen, high-pitched cry → call provider
  • Avoid direct sunlight through windows — not effective and risks overheating
  • Follow-up: recheck bilirubin within 24–48h after discharge per provider order
