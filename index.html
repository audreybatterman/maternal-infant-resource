Maternal Health

🌸 Mom
Mnemonic: B-U-B-B-L-E-H-E — systematic head-to-toe postpartum assessment

B — Breasts

  • Softness, engorgement, symmetry
  • Nipple condition: cracks, flat, inverted
  • Colostrum/milk presence
  • Breast/latching education offered

U — Uterus

  • Fundal height (fingerbreadths below umbilicus)
  • Firmness: boggy → massage needed
  • Midline position
  • Day 1: at umbilicus; involutes ~1 cm/day
⚠ Alert: Boggy uterus → assess bladder, then fundal massage. Report excessive bleeding immediately.

B — Bowel

  • Bowel sounds in all 4 quadrants
  • Last BM documented
  • Stool softeners ordered/given?
  • Passing flatus; no distension

B — Bladder

  • Voiding adequately (≥150 mL per void)
  • Bladder not distended above symphysis
  • U/O ≥30 mL/hr (post-Foley)
  • Pain/burning with urination

L — Lochia

  • Rubra (red,1–3d) → Serosa (pink/brown,4–10d) → Alba (cream,10–14d)
  • Amount: scant/light/moderate/heavy/excessive
  • Foul odor = infection concern
  • Clots >golf ball = report

E — Episiotomy / Perineum

  • REEDA: Redness, Edema, Ecchymosis, Discharge, Approximation
  • Ice pack first 24h; sitz bath after
  • Intact sutures (if present)
  • Hemorrhoids noted

H — Homans / Lower Extremities

  • Calf pain on dorsiflexion (Homans sign — limited sensitivity)
  • Leg edema, warmth, erythema
  • DVT risk: early ambulation, SCDs

E — Emotional / Education

  • Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS)
  • Baby blues vs. PPD distinction
  • Support system, bonding observed
  • Discharge education completed

Pain Management

  • Ibuprofen 600 mg PO q6h (preferred)
  • Acetaminophen 650–1000 mg PO q6h PRN
  • Ice perineum first 24h; sitz bath after
  • Tucks pads / Dermoplast spray
  • Afterpains: worse with multiparas + breastfeeding

Routine Medications

💊 Prenatal vitamin🩸 Iron supplement💉 Pitocin IV🟡 Stool softener🛡 Rhogam (Rh neg)💉 Rubella (non-immune)

Hemorrhage Prevention

  • Fundal massage if boggy
  • Oxytocin (Pitocin) — standard uterotonic
  • Methergine — NOT with HTN
  • Hemabate — NOT with asthma
  • Monitor CBC, report Hgb <8 g/dL

Discharge Teaching

  • Pelvic rest × 6 weeks
  • No heavy lifting (>10 lb) × 4–6 weeks
  • Fever >100.4°F (38°C), foul lochia, wound opening → call OB
  • Signs of PPD; 24/7 support line provided
  • OB follow-up: 3 wks C/S or 6 wks vaginal
  • Contraception counseling

Key Teaching

  • Feed every 1.5–3h, 8–12×/day newborn
  • Alternate breasts; fully empty one side first
  • Nipple pain is NOT normal; reassess latch
  • Mastitis: flu-like + unilateral breast warmth/redness

Head-to-Toe

  • Head: fontanelles, molding, caput/cephalhematoma
  • Eyes: sclera clear, red reflex bilateral
  • Mouth: intact palate, Epstein pearls (normal), sucking reflex
  • Clavicles: palpate for fracture (esp. shoulder dystocia)
  • Chest: equal breath sounds, no retractions/grunting
  • Abdomen: cord 2 arteries + 1 vein; soft, no masses
  • Genitalia: normal for GA, patent anus
  • Hips: Ortolani/Barlow — no clunk/click
  • Skin: color, vernix, Mongolian spots (document!)

Newborn Screenings

  • Metabolic screen (PKU card) — at 24h or before discharge
  • Hearing screen (UNHS) — before discharge
  • Critical CHD: pulse ox pre/post ductal at 24h+
  • Bilirubin — TCB or serum per protocol

Weight Changes

  • Expected loss: up to 7–10% birth weight in first 3–5 days
  • >10% loss = evaluate feeding, consider supplementation
  • Regain birth weight by day 10–14
  • Gaining ~20–30 g/day after transitional loss

Safe Sleep (AAP)

  • Back to sleep, EVERY time — supine
  • Firm, flat surface (approved crib/bassinet only)
  • Room-sharing (not bed-sharing) ≥6 months
  • No soft objects, pillows, bumpers, loose bedding
  • Pacifier at sleep time (after BF established)
⚠ SIDS: "Back to sleep, tummy to play." Document safe sleep teaching every shift.

Jaundice

  • Physiologic: appears day 2–3, peaks day 3–5, resolves by day 14
  • Pathologic: appears <24h → investigate immediately
  • Cephalocaudal progression: face → chest → abdomen → legs
  • Treatment: phototherapy, hydration

Thermoregulation

  • Newborns cannot shiver — prone to heat loss
  • 4 mechanisms: Evaporation, Radiation, Conduction, Convection
  • Skin-to-skin = best thermoregulation
  • Target axillary: 97.7–99.5°F (36.5–37.5°C)

Routine Meds at Birth

💉 Erythromycin eye ointment 💉 Vitamin K 1 mg IM 🛡 Hep B vaccine (if consent)
